Hermes for the Host Network — Executive Brief

The whole case on a page — for the owner-operator of a host network of independent luxury advisors.

01EXECUTIVE BRIEF — HERMES FOR THE NETWORK

Give every advisor an AI chief-of-staff they own. Give the network the integrity it's missing.

Hermes-orchestrated, Claude-powered agents that draft in each advisor's voice, watch every active trip, and reconcile every commission — advisor-owned, host-invisible, exportable. The agent drafts; the advisor sends. Nothing autonomous ever touches a client or a dollar.

The problem, in one sentence: Across the network, commission you've already earned leaks out unclaimed, advisors burn premium hours on admin instead of clients, and warm leads go cold — while you carry compliance and duty-of-care risk with no shared system of record.

The solution, in one sentence: A four-plane Hermes brain that automates the scaffolding and never the relationship — drafting, monitoring, and reconciling for each advisor, while a human approves everything that moves money or makes a promise.

05THREE NON-NEGOTIABLE GUARDRAILS

Trust is the product

  • A human sends every client message. The agent drafts; the advisor sends. No autonomous client-facing message anywhere — backed by an immutable audit log and external penetration testing.
  • Structured, curated memory — never a free-text dump. Per-client facts live in structured SQLite/Supabase; hard constraints (allergies, do-not-book, mobility) are human-curated, and any inferred fact is flagged for confirmation before use.
  • Self-hosted, PII-redacted, boundary-enforced. Deterministic redaction before any external call, a scoped Tailscale network, an immutable audit log, and an external security review — boundaries enforced structurally at the ZeroID/MCP layer (default-deny, time-boxed, row-level), never by the agent policing itself.
Verify everything that moves money or makes a promise. Automate the scaffolding; never the relationship.
06WHO GETS WHAT — & WHY IT HOLDS UP

Advisors own it. The network gets integrity.

Each advisor gets

An agent that makes them money and saves them time — in their own voice (opt-in, never mandated). The client dossier and entire personal pipeline are advisor-owned, per-tenant BYOK-encrypted, and host-invisible, with one-click machine-readable export. Your clients are yours.

The host network gets

Compliance and duty-of-care (pre-trip, disruption and passport sentinels), commission integrity (one engine; each advisor sees only their slice), continuity via hot-handoff before any access is revoked, and a stickier network — without expropriating a single advisor.

Proof points:

  • The highest-confidence wins are deterministic, not magic — commission reconciliation, confirmation capture and passport math are rules-based; the AI only drafts, humans verify. High confidence
  • Boundaries live in the architecture, not in promises: four planes with scoped, time-boxed ZeroID tokens — the host operator cannot read advisor plaintext.
  • Running cost is trivial (~$4.6K/yr) thanks to prompt caching; the real investment is the one-time build, which you phase.
  • 1099 status is preserved: mandates are confined to compliance, duty-of-care and financial rails — never voice or workflow.
Launch requires an ICA addendum settling dossier ownership, new-IP (the AI-enriched dossier) ownership, and portability — agreed before any data accumulates.
07THE DECISION

Approve a phased Phase-1 pilot.

Don't fund all 18 tools up front. If Phase 1 clears — and the modeling says it pays for itself in year one — we scale per advisor across the network.

Recommendation: proceed to a 60-day Phase-1 pilot. Low cash at risk, human-in-the-loop throughout, advisor-owned by default, and the one leak with the hardest dollars — unpaid commission — fixed first.
