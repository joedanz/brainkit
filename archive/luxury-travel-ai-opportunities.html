01Executive Summary

Hermes as the brain. Your travel stack as the load-bearing infrastructure.

The opportunity is the connective intelligence no SaaS spans: per-client nuance no CRM schema captures, your house voice on every draft, and 24/7 proactive monitoring of named clients.

Three leaks dominate a boutique practice. Growth leaks as leads scatter across WhatsApp, DMs, SMS and email and go cold, stalled proposals are never chased, and warm high-LTV clients quietly go dormant. Time leaks as bespoke design and proposal assembly devour unbilled hours and confirmation data is re-keyed across disconnected systems all day. Money & trust leak as commissions arrive late or short-paid, a single missed supplier cut-off costs more than the planning fee, unapplied VIP perks erode trust, and a missed passport-validity rule becomes a relationship-ending liability.

Three guardrails are non-negotiable and baked into every idea. Human-in-the-loop on every external client message — the agent drafts, the advisor sends. Structured SQLite/Supabase memory, never flat markdown, with hard constraints human-curated rather than auto-ingested. And self-hosting with deterministic PII redaction, a scoped network, an immutable audit log, and an external security review.

Two principles govern scope. Automate the scaffolding, never the relationship — the agent removes grunt work so the advisor has more time for the human magic. And verify everything that moves money or makes a promise: live jet, villa and restaurant inventory is largely not scrapable, so all rates, availability and bookings are confirmed with the supplier by the advisor. The agent never tells a client a fix is in place before it is actually held.

The plan is sequenced by blast radius. Phase 1 ships internal-only, deterministic wins with zero client exposure and stands up a mandatory shadow-mode pilot with quantitative go/no-go thresholds. Phase 2 automates the core grind as advisor-augmentation. Phase 3 promotes the supervised, draft-first differentiation clients actually feel. Be honest about platform risk — Hermes has no SLA, is self-modifying and painful to self-host — so reserve it for judgment-based steps, use deterministic automation for fixed triggers, and commit an owner for security and uptime.

03The non-negotiables

Three guardrails make this safe for UHNW clients.

An audit of the platform found approval-gate violations in 112 of 129 sessions. In this business a single visible error is reputational. So the rules come first — and they are engineered, not assumed.

Rule 01 ✍️

The advisor always sends

Human-in-the-loop on every external client message — not just money and bookings. The agent drafts and monitors; the advisor reviews and sends. No autonomous client-facing message anywhere, backed by an immutable audit log.

Rule 02 🧠

Memory you can trust

Structured SQLite/Supabase memory, never flat markdown. Hard constraints — allergies, do-not-book lists, mobility — are human-curated, never auto-ingested. A hallucinated allergy or invented preference is unacceptable here.

Rule 03 🔐

Sovereign by design

Self-hosted on agency infrastructure with deterministic PII redaction, a scoped Tailscale network, an immutable audit log, and an external pen-test — local-model redaction alone is not sufficient for passports, schedules and home addresses.

08Hermes vs. the alternatives

It's not Hermes or the best tool. It's Hermes on top of them.

The honest verdict

Hermes is the differentiating second brain — unstructured per-client memory no CRM captures, drafting in each advisor's voice, proactive monitoring of named clients, orchestration across your stack via MCP. The travel SaaS, GDS and consortium are the load-bearing infrastructure: inventory, money, compliance, perks and polished deliverables. Do not make Hermes your booking engine, commission ledger or system of record — and only self-host if someone owns security and uptime.
