Trust & Data-Governance Playbook

Trust & Data-Governance Playbook — HNW Concierge Agent on Hermes

The document you show a security-conscious family office — independent-contractor-ownership-aware, portability-first, and audit-ready.

01WHY THIS DOCUMENT EXISTS

Trust is the product. This is how we make it defensible.

This playbook governs an AI concierge built on the Hermes platform for an invitation-only travel atelier staffed by 1099 independent-contractor advisors. It is the document we hand a security-conscious family office to prove three things: their dossier never leaks, no machine ever speaks to them or moves their money on its own authority, and the advisor — not the host — owns the relationship.

The whole design rests on one inversion: everything defaults to advisor-owned, per-tenant encrypted, and host-invisible. The agency earns each act of sharing; it never expropriates. Guardrails are enforced structurally at the identity and tool layer (default-deny, time-boxed, row-level scope) — never via the agent policing itself, because a model under context compression drops constraints around turn ~30 and approval-gate adherence measured ~112/129 when left to prose alone.

BYOKper-tenant keys the host cannot read in plaintext
1-clickmachine-readable dossier export — "your clients are yours"
Core invariant. The agent drafts, stages, and recommends. A human sends, confirms, and spends. Automate the scaffolding; never automate the relationship. Verify everything that moves money or makes a promise.
Why structural, not behavioural. SOUL.md voice rules and MCP tool-whitelists (draft-only Gmail, GDS with create_order/issue_ticket/cancel_order excluded) are necessary but not sufficient. The hard stops live in the network, the token scopes, and the database — places the model cannot talk its way past.
02THE FOUR-PLANE ARCHITECTURE

Isolation is a topology, not a promise

Four planes, each a separate trust boundary. Crossing a boundary requires a freshly minted, scoped, time-boxed ZeroID token. There is no ambient authority and no plane can widen its own scope.

T0

Tier-0 — Agency Brain hermes profile (institutional)

Institutional data only: the firm-negotiated supplier layer, reliability scores, escalation/discretion policy, compliance rails. It physically holds the advisor-owned dossiers per-tenant encrypted and cannot read them in plaintext — it is storage, not an audience. No house voice lives here.

T1

Tier-1 — Chief-of-Staff Orchestrator gateway + cron + ZeroID minter

Routes work, mints scoped tokens, enforces every approval gate, runs institutional cron (duty-of-care sentinels). It is the only component that issues authority, and it issues the minimum, for the shortest window, against named rows.

T2

Tier-2 — Personal Advisor Agents ~/.hermes/profiles/<advisor>/

One per advisor, each with its own SOUL.md voice, own profile, own state.db, and visibility into only its assigned clients. Sibling advisors are mutually invisible. The Principal-Owner Personal Agent sits here too — walled from the admin hat, dual-controlled break-glass, with an externally-anchored, principal-uneditable audit trail.

T3

Tier-3 — Specialist Sub-agent Pool ephemeral

Delegated, short-lived workers (research, document parsing). They receive ephemeral, PII-stripped task payloads and return de-identified write-backs. They never hold a long-lived dossier handle and die at task end.

Ownership tier tags used throughout: AGENCY firm-owned and scaled · SPLIT shared, with advisor-brought assets returning on exit · HYBRID shared back-end, personal voice, dossier defaults advisor-owned.
03DATA CLASSIFICATION TIERS

Three tiers, handling rules per tier

Every field in the dossier maps to exactly one tier. The tier dictates encryption, who may see it, whether it may leave the boundary, and what redaction must happen first.

RESTRICTED Max controls

Passport & visa numbers, national IDs, card/payment patterns, known-traveler IDs, biometrics, real-time and home locations, security/medical/protective-detail facts, exact DOB. Source columns: documents.doc_number_enc, clients.known_traveler_ids, hard_constraints (medical/security).

CONFIDENTIAL High

Soft preferences, itineraries & bookings, trip occasions, loyalty programs, supplier net rates, commission figures, household relationship tier, discretion notes. The "what we do for them," not the "who they legally are."

INTERNAL Baseline

Aggregated, k-anonymised benchmarks; de-identified supplier reliability scores; non-attributed operational metrics; firm policy documents. Nothing that re-identifies a household.

RESTRICTED — at rest
Per-tenant BYOK envelope encryption. Document numbers as ciphertext only (doc_number_enc), never plaintext. Host operator cannot decrypt.
RESTRICTED — in transit
Never leaves the boundary in clear. Stripped/tokenised by the redaction pipeline (§04) before any external API call. Never echoed back into a channel where it is not already present.
RESTRICTED — access
Tier-2 owning advisor + dual-control break-glass only. Never to specialist sub-agents. Never to the host roll-up. Read events always audited.
CONFIDENTIAL — at rest
Per-tenant encrypted; structured rows in the dossier DB, never flat markdown. Inferred facts flagged unconfirmed until a human confirms.
CONFIDENTIAL — sharing
Cross-advisor sharing OFF by default. May enter a shared write only PII-stripped and via transactional confirmation-to-CRM capture.
INTERNAL — sharing
May aggregate firm-wide only above a k-anonymity floor (k≥5 households). Never reconstructs an individual book.
Hard-constraint provenance. Allergies, do-not-book, mobility, security and budget ceilings are human-curated in hard_constraints, never auto-ingested. Inferred candidates are written as unconfirmed soft_preferences and surfaced for confirmation — they never silently become hard rules.
04DETERMINISTIC PII REDACTION

Nothing Restricted crosses the wire un-tokenised

Redaction is deterministic-first: a regex + dictionary pass that fails closed, with a local-model NER pass as a secondary net — never the local model alone. Substitution is reversible only inside the boundary via a local vault map; the external provider sees placeholders.

  1. Dictionary pass (highest precision). Pull exact client legal/preferred names, property & supplier names, emails and phones from the dossier for the in-scope rows and replace them first. Known strings beat pattern-matching.
  2. Regex pass (deterministic primary). Apply the pattern library below; PAN candidates must pass Luhn before redaction to cut false positives.
  3. Local-model NER pass (secondary recall net). A local model flags residual person/location/org spans the patterns missed. It can only add redactions, never remove them — it cannot un-mask.
  4. Tokenise & vault. Replace each hit with a typed placeholder ([PASSPORT_1], [CLIENT_2]); store the mapping in a boundary-local vault keyed to the request id.
  5. Egress assertion (fail-closed). If any Restricted pattern still matches the outbound payload, the call is blocked, not sent. No silent best-effort.
  6. Re-hydrate on return. Map placeholders back to real values locally so the advisor sees a complete draft; the provider never did.
// Deterministic redaction pattern library (primary pass)
EMAIL        => [A-Za-z0-9._%+-]+@[A-Za-z0-9.-]+\.[A-Za-z]{2,}
PHONE_E164   => \+?[1-9]\d{0,2}[\s.\-]?\(?\d{1,4}\)?[\s.\-]?\d{3,4}[\s.\-]?\d{3,4}
PAN_CARD     => \b(?:\d[ \-]?){13,19}\b        # then require Luhn() == true
PASSPORT     => \b[A-Z]{1,2}\d{6,9}\b           # generic MRZ-style doc number
US_SSN       => \b\d{3}-\d{2}-\d{4}\b
IBAN         => \b[A-Z]{2}\d{2}[A-Z0-9]{11,30}\b
KTN_GLOBAL   => \b\d{9,15}\b                     # known-traveler / PASSID (context-gated)
DOB_ISO      => \b(19|20)\d{2}-(0[1-9]|1[0-2])-(0[1-9]|[12]\d|3[01])\b
GEO_COORD    => [-+]?\d{1,2}\.\d{3,}\s*,\s*[-+]?\d{1,3}\.\d{3,}
// Dictionary terms (exact-match, from dossier rows in scope):
//   client legal_full_name, preferred_name, primary_email, primary_phone,
//   household.name, supplier/property names, emergency_contact
// Decision: regex_or_dict_hit OR local_ner_hit  =>  REDACT
// Egress gate: if any RESTRICTED pattern survives  =>  BLOCK send, raise incident
Context-gated numerics. Bare digit runs (KTN, loyalty numbers, DOB) only trigger when near a trigger keyword ("passport", "DOB", "Global Entry", "card") to avoid shredding flight numbers and prices. The local-model pass backstops anything the gate misses.
Self-hosted by design. The pipeline runs inside the scoped network on owned hardware (Pi / Mac mini / VPS). Frontier models are reached only with tokenised payloads; cheap local models handle routing and the NER net. Determinism is what makes redaction auditable.
05ACCESS CONTROL & NETWORK MODEL

Least-privilege at the wire, the token, and the row

A scoped Tailscale tailnet carries all internal traffic; ACL tags express who may reach what. On top of the network, ZeroID mints short-lived, single-purpose tokens with row-level scope. There is no path to prod that bypasses both.

tag:agency-brain
Tier-0 store. Accepts connections only from tag:orchestrator. Holds encrypted dossiers; serves ciphertext + decrypt-on-token only.
tag:orchestrator
Tier-1. May reach brain + advisor agents + MCP egress proxy. Sole minter of scoped tokens. Runs cron.
tag:advisor-<name>
One tag per advisor agent. Reaches only its own profile store and the orchestrator. Cannot see tag:advisor-* siblings.
tag:specialist
Ephemeral pool. Egress to web/MCP only via the redaction proxy; no direct dossier route.
tag:mcp-egress
The only node allowed outbound to GDS/Gmail/CRM/search. All outbound passes the redaction gate first.
tag:admin (break-glass)
Human operators. No standing access to plaintext dossiers. Dual-control, time-boxed, fully audited.
  • Default-deny everywhere. Messaging gateway is allowlist-only (e.g. TELEGRAM_ALLOWED_USERS); DM pairing codes expire in 1 hour. Unknown senders get nothing.
  • Time-boxed tokens. Every cross-plane grant expires (minutes, not days) and names the exact rows/operations it permits. No wildcard scopes.
  • No SSH or self-modify into prod without a gate. Hermes can self-modify; in prod that path is closed. Code/config changes go through review + a human break-glass approval; the terminal backend in prod is locked to a non-self-modifying profile.
  • Write-path minimalism. Of the institutional cron jobs, exactly one writes (supplier-reliability refresh). Everything else is read + draft.
  • External security review. Annual external pen-test of the tailnet, ZeroID scopes, redaction gate, and break-glass path; findings tracked to closure.
Host cannot read into a tenant. ACL tags plus per-tenant BYOK mean even a fully compromised admin node yields ciphertext, not a family office's itinerary. Decryption requires a token the orchestrator will only mint to the owning advisor agent.
06IMMUTABLE AUDIT LOG

Append-only, hash-chained, externally anchored

The dossier ships an append-only audit_log with before/after JSON on every sensitive mutation (bookings, commission, documents, hard_constraints), one row per real change via WHEN-guards. We harden it into a tamper-evident chain and anchor it outside the host so no operator — including the principal — can rewrite history.

id
Monotonic sequence (AUTOINCREMENT) — gap detection.
actor
hermes-agent · advisor handle · system — who acted.
action / entity_table / entity_id
insert · update · delete · custom, and the exact row touched.
before_json / after_json
State snapshots (NULL on insert/delete respectively).
reason
Agent-supplied rationale; e.g. "HARD CONSTRAINT REMOVED — review required".
session_ref
Hermes session id, linking back to the FTS5 session store.
prev_hash / row_hash
Added: SHA-256 of (prev_hash ‖ canonical row). Breaks if any prior row is altered.
anchor_ref
Added: periodic chain head written to external WORM storage; principal-uneditable copy of the head.
created_at
ISO-8601 UTC, DB-stamped.
  • No UPDATE or DELETE grants on audit_log — insert-only at the DB role level, not just by convention.
  • WHEN-guards keep exactly one audit row per genuine change (the updated_at self-UPDATE fires sibling triggers; guards suppress the duplicate). Re-test if recursive_triggers is ever turned ON.
  • Hash-chain head exported on each tick to off-host WORM; mismatch on re-verification = tamper alarm to the escalation channel.
  • Restricted-field reads (decrypt events) are logged too, not just writes.
  • The Principal-Owner agent's actions write to an externally-anchored, principal-uneditable log — the owner cannot launder their own break-glass use.
07RETENTION, DELETION & RIGHT-TO-BE-FORGOTTEN

Keep the minimum, delete on request, prove the audit survives

Retention is purpose-bound and tier-bound. Erasure is real (crypto-shred the tenant key where possible), but the immutable audit trail is preserved in tamper-evident, minimised form because deletion itself must be provable.

Active dossier (Confidential)
Retained while the relationship is active + the advisor's chosen window. Advisor-owned; advisor sets the clock.
Restricted identifiers
Document numbers kept only as ciphertext, only as long as a live trip needs them; purged after trip completion unless the client opts to retain.
Financial / commission records
Retained to satisfy tax/accounting duties (typically up to 7 yrs), then purged. PCI: no card data retained at all.
Session / state.db transcripts
Rolling window with compression; sensitive spans redacted before long-term retention. Never the canonical store of hard facts.
Audit log
Retained beyond record deletion in minimised form (ids + hashes + action), so an erasure is itself auditable.
  1. Receive & verify. Erasure/access request arrives; verify identity through the advisor (the controller for that dossier — see §09).
  2. Scope. Resolve all rows across clients, trips, bookings, documents, preferences, search index, and cron outputs.
  3. Crypto-shred. Where a tenant/sub-key isolates the subject, destroy the key — ciphertext becomes unrecoverable instantly.
  4. Hard-delete residue. Remove plaintext rows and FTS entries; cron output markdown purged.
  5. Preserve the proof. Audit log retains a minimised, hash-chained record that deletion occurred, by whom, when.
  6. Confirm. Advisor confirms completion to the client; legal-hold exceptions (live dispute/regulatory) are documented, not silently ignored.
Backups honour erasure. Hermes' backups/ state-snapshots/ checkpoints/ are on the same rotation; a subject is removed from restorable snapshots within the documented backup-cycle window, and any restore re-applies pending erasures.
08COMPLIANCE STANCE — GDPR / CCPA / PCI

One posture, mapped per regime

We engineer to the strictest applicable bar and map it down. Data minimisation, purpose limitation, lawful basis, and breach duties are designed in, not bolted on.

GDPR EU/UK clients

Lawful basis recorded per processing purpose. Data-subject rights (access, rectification, erasure, portability) are productised (§07, §12). Data minimisation via redaction. 72-hour breach notification to the supervisory authority; affected individuals notified without undue delay when high risk. DPIA on file for HNW profiling risk.

CCPA / CPRA CA residents

Right to know, delete, correct, and opt-out of "sale/share" — which we do not do: no dossier is sold or cross-context shared. No financial-incentive discrimination for exercising rights. Service-provider terms flow down to every sub-processor (GDS, Gmail, CRM, search).

PCI-DSS Payments

No payment-pattern storage. PAN/CVV never persist — not in the dossier, not in state.db, not in transcripts; the redaction PAN+Luhn rule blocks them at egress and ingress. Payments are handed to a PCI-compliant processor; we keep tokens/references only. Scope-reduction by never touching cardholder data.

  • Records of Processing Activities (RoPA) maintained; sub-processor list published to clients on request.
  • Cross-border transfers covered by SCCs / adequacy; self-hosting keeps data resident where contracted.
  • Breach-notification clock starts at awareness; the incident runbook (§11) drives the timeline and the controller/processor handoff (§09).
  • Children's data and special-category data (health constraints) handled under elevated basis and access.
09CONTROLLER vs PROCESSOR — HOST & IC

The advisor is the controller; the host is the processor

Because dossiers are advisor-owned, the independent-contractor advisor is the data controller for their clients. The host operates the platform as a processor on the advisor's documented instructions. This division is the legal spine of the 1099 model and must be written before data accumulates.

Advisor = Controller
Determines purposes and means for their clients' data; holds the client relationship and the lawful basis; is the first responder to data-subject requests.
Host = Processor
Provides infrastructure, encryption, redaction, audit, duty-of-care sentinels; acts only on instructions; cannot read tenant plaintext; cannot repurpose data.
Shared institutional layer
For firm-owned data (supplier reliability, escalation policy) the host is an independent controller; the k-anonymity floor keeps individuals out of it.
Sub-processors
GDS, Gmail, CRM, search — engaged with flow-down DPA terms; listed and change-notified.
Passport / PII breach handling. On a Restricted-data breach: the host (processor) must notify the affected advisor (controller) without undue delay on becoming aware. The host contains and forensically preserves (audit chain, redaction logs); the advisor as controller decides and issues regulator/data-subject notifications inside the GDPR 72-hour window. Crypto-shred limits blast radius — exfiltrated ciphertext without the tenant key is not a plaintext breach, which is documented in the DPIA as a mitigating control.
Written, not assumed. A Data Processing Agreement (host↔advisor) plus the ICA Addendum (§12) record these roles, sub-processor flow-down, breach timelines, and the host's no-plaintext-access commitment.
10HUMAN-ESCALATION MATRIX

Trigger → required action

Escalation is an AGENCY-owned protocol AGENCY — uniform across every advisor, enforced at the tool layer. The agent never decides it is fine to proceed past one of these.

Emotional / distressed client
Stop drafting auto-replies. Surface verbatim to the owning advisor immediately; agent suggests tone, human responds. No templated empathy sent autonomously.
Complaint or service failure
Flag to advisor + log an incident (§11). Draft a recovery option set; advisor approves and sends. Never minimise or close on the agent's authority.
Crisis / duty-of-care (disruption, medical, security, safety)
Page the assigned advisor and the escalation channel. Duty-of-care sentinels keep running; the agent assembles facts and options, a human directs action.
VIP / spend threshold crossed
Above the household's configured threshold (or any private_office / premier tier), require explicit advisor sign-off before any external step.
Money or booking
Always human. No confirm, ticket, deposit release, fund move, or commission action without explicit approval. GDS order/ticket/cancel tools are excluded at the MCP layer; payments draft-only.
Ambiguity / unverifiable fact
One well-judged clarifying question beats a confident wrong action. If price, availability, or a visa/passport rule cannot be verified, state what is known, what is not, and stop.
Hard-constraint conflict
Halt and surface. Never "work around" an allergy, medical, accessibility, security, or budget-ceiling constraint.
11CLIENT-INCIDENT RUNBOOK

Detect → Contain → Correct → Log → Learn

A single, AGENCY-owned loop AGENCY for any incident touching a client — data, service, or safety. The human leads; the agent assembles.

  1. Detect. Source: sentinel alert, advisor report, client complaint, redaction-gate block, or audit-chain mismatch. Open an incident record with severity and affected household(s).
  2. Contain. Stop the bleed: revoke the implicated token, pause the relevant cron job, quarantine the affected agent profile, freeze outbound drafts. For data incidents, crypto-shred or isolate exposed material.
  3. Correct. Human-led remediation: fix the booking, re-issue the document request, correct the dossier row (audited), and prepare client-facing recovery for advisor approval.
  4. Log. Full timeline into the immutable audit log + incident record: what happened, what data, what tier, who was notified, controller/processor handoff (§09), regulator clock if triggered.
  5. Learn. Post-incident review → update redaction patterns, token scopes, escalation thresholds, or SOUL.md guidance. Capture the pattern so the same class of incident cannot recur. Feed material findings to the next external review.
Notification timing. For Restricted-data incidents the GDPR 72-hour and CCPA duties begin at awareness — the runbook's Detect step timestamps awareness, and Contain/Log run in parallel with the controller's notification decision, never after it.
12OWNERSHIP & PORTABILITY

Your clients are yours — proven, not promised

This is the IC value proposition and the reason a top advisor brings their book. The dossier and the entire personal pipeline default to advisor-owned, per-tenant BYOK-encrypted, host-invisible, with one-click export built as a product feature.

Advisor-owned by default HYBRID

Dossier, personal-channel lead capture, buying-signal, referral, occasion, retention, and house-voice all default to the advisor. Segregated per-tenant, encrypted with the advisor's own key. The host runs the back-end but is not an audience.

BYOK the host cannot read Must-do

Per-tenant keys held by the advisor; the operator stores only ciphertext. No standing decrypt path for admins. "Host-invisible" is enforced by key custody, not policy text.

One-click export Product feature

Machine-readable export of the full dossier + AI-enrichments on demand — portable, complete, no friction. Portability is a feature, not a favour.

Advisor-brought suppliers return on exit SPLIT

Firm-negotiated supplier layer scales for everyone, but advisor-introduced contacts stay advisor-tagged and return with the advisor on exit. The firm keeps its own contracts, not the advisor's relationships.

ICA Addendum — sign before data accumulates. The Independent-Contractor Agreement addendum settles, up front: (1) dossier ownership = advisor; (2) NEW-IP ownership — the AI-enriched dossier (inferred preferences, embeddings, reliability context layered on the advisor's data) is the advisor's, not a host asset; (3) portability mechanics and format; (4) supplier return-on-exit; (5) the host's no-plaintext-access and processor role. Settling this after data accumulates is how trust dies.
Promotion is opt-in, hard-gated. House-voice sharing and fact-promotion (one advisor's learned fact feeding the firm) are STRICTLY OPT-IN at the tool layer. Non-response = no action. The cross-advisor promotion engine is OFF by default. De-identify-before-write and a k-anonymity floor (k≥5) apply to every shared benchmark.
131099-STATUS PRESERVATION

Mandate the rails, never the relationship

The architecture must not create an ABC-test / AB5 misclassification risk. We confine agency mandates to compliance, duty-of-care, and financial rails, and we never dictate how an advisor sells or sounds.

  • Mandates are limited to: compliance (privacy, PCI, breach), duty-of-care (pre-trip, disruption, passport/visa logic), and financial rails (commission integrity, no auto-pay). Nothing about how to serve.
  • No mandated voice or workflow. SOUL.md voice is the advisor's; the firm supplies a default the advisor may discard. Pipeline and cadence are advisor-chosen.
  • No auto-widened scope. An advisor's agent cannot have its visibility or duties silently expanded by the host. Scope changes require the advisor's act.
  • Kill switch = duty-of-care continuity, not control. The only firm override exists to keep a client safe mid-trip (hot-handoff, §14) — never to discipline, observe, or redirect an advisor's business.
  • No host surveillance of the book. The host roll-up brief is excluded or aggregate-only (§15); the firm cannot watch an IC's clients or HNW travel patterns.
Test it against the factors. Each mandate is documented with its duty-of-care or legal justification, so that under an ABC/AB5 review the firm's control is visibly confined to outcomes the law itself requires — not to the manner and means of the advisor's work.
14ADVISOR-DEPARTURE RUNBOOK

Hot-handoff before any token is revoked

When an advisor leaves, duty-of-care to their clients must never lapse mid-trip, and their ownership rights must be honoured. Coverage comes first; revocation comes last.

  1. Export & handover (advisor's rights first). Trigger one-click export to the departing advisor; confirm dossier + NEW-IP delivered per the ICA Addendum; advisor-brought suppliers re-tagged for return.
  2. Identify live duty-of-care. Enumerate in-progress and imminent trips (sentinels: pre-trip, disruption, passport/visa) that cannot go dark.
  3. Hot-handoff. Assign a covering advisor; mint new scoped tokens to the covering Tier-2 agent for the affected clients before touching the departing one. Verify the covering agent can see what it needs.
  4. Confirm continuity. Prove sentinels fire under the new owner — a test tick on the covered trips — so no gap exists.
  5. Revoke. Only now revoke the departing advisor's prod tokens, tailnet tags, and gateway access. Decommission the profile.
  6. Settle & record. Final commission reconciliation on the advisor's attributed slice; departure logged to the immutable audit chain.
Ordering is the control. Revoking before hot-handoff completes would orphan a family mid-trip. The runbook makes coverage a precondition of revocation — the kill switch protects clients, it does not punish advisors.
15FORBIDDEN ACTIONS

Lines the system will not cross — for the agent and the host

These are enforced structurally (token scope, MCP whitelist, network ACL), not merely instructed. Each is a hard stop.

The agent must never:

  • Send any client-facing message autonomously. Draft only; a human presses send.
  • Auto-pay or auto-book — confirm a reservation, issue a ticket, release a deposit, or move funds. (GDS create_order/issue_ticket/cancel_order excluded; payments draft-only.)
  • Covertly profile a client from public signals or scrape social/web footprints to enrich a dossier without consent.
  • Mark a passport, visa, or entry requirement as "cleared" / "valid" — it states what is known and unverified; a human confirms travel eligibility.
  • Run LinkedIn or social automation — no connection requests, scraping, messaging, or bulk outreach.
  • Store passport or card numbers in plaintext, or echo Restricted identifiers into a channel where they are not already present.
  • Promote one advisor's facts to the firm, or alter SOUL.md voice, without an explicit opt-in (non-response = no action).

The host operator must never:

  • Read an IC's dossier in plaintext — BYOK + ACL make this technically impossible without dual-control break-glass, which is itself audited.
  • Perform silent fact-promotion or cross-advisor sharing — the promotion engine is OFF by default and opt-in only.
  • Generate a roll-up of individual books — no host view of an IC's clients or HNW travel patterns; firm analytics are aggregate-only above the k-anonymity floor.
  • Auto-widen an advisor's scope, mandate their voice/workflow, or use the kill switch for anything other than duty-of-care continuity.
  • SSH or self-modify into prod without the review + break-glass gate.
Why a list, not a vibe. Every line maps to a concrete enforcement point: an excluded MCP tool, an absent token scope, a denied tailnet route, or a missing decrypt key. The agent cannot reason its way around an authority it was never granted.
16INSURANCE & CLIENT DISCLOSURE

E&O coverage and informed consent

Defensibility includes the paperwork. We carry appropriate coverage and we tell clients, plainly, that an AI assists their advisor — and what it will and will not do.

E&O / Professional Liability Must-do

Errors & Omissions cover written to explicitly contemplate AI-assisted drafting and operations. Confirm the policy does not exclude AI-tool use; clarify host-vs-IC coverage so a 1099 advisor is not uninsured for agency-provided tooling.

Cyber / Data-Breach High

Coverage for breach response, notification costs, and forensics on Restricted data. Aligns to the §11 runbook and §09 controller/processor split so claims map cleanly to responsibility.

Client disclosure

At onboarding, clients are told: an AI concierge supports your advisor; it drafts and prepares; a human always sends, confirms, and spends; your data is encrypted and owned by your advisor; here is how to exercise your rights.

Consent & basis

Explicit consent recorded for processing and for any optional enrichment. Hard constraints captured with the client's confirmation. Opt-out of optional features is honoured without service penalty (CCPA non-discrimination).

Disclosure is standing
Re-surfaced on material changes (new sub-processor, new capability), not buried once at signup.
Consent is granular
Core duty-of-care vs optional enrichment/promotion are separately consented; the latter default OFF.
Liability follows control
Because a human approves every external act, the human-in-the-loop record (audit log) is the evidence that no machine acted alone.
The whole posture, in one line: automate the scaffolding, never the relationship; verify everything that moves money or makes a promise; and make the advisor — not the host — the owner of the trust that is the product.
