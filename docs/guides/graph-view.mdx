Every compiled vault is a web of notes joined by `[[wikilinks]]`. There are two
ways to see that web as an interactive graph, depending on how you use your
vault.

## The dashboard's Graph tab

The [live dashboard](/guides/reference-deployment) has a **Graph** tab: your
notes as glowing points, colored by space, linked where notes reference each
other. It works for everyone — including people who only ever talk to their
assistant in chat and never touch files, since the dashboard is just a URL.

- **Hover** a note to light up its neighborhood; everything else recedes.
- **Zoom in** and note titles fade into view.
- **Click** a note to list what it links to and what links back, and open it
  in the Query tab.
- The **control panel** on the canvas filters by space, hides or shows
  orphan notes, and tunes size, link distance, and layout forces. Your
  settings stick across visits.
- **3D view** renders the same graph as a constellation you can orbit.

The graph updates live: add a note or a link and it springs into place without
losing your zoom or layout.

## Obsidian's graph view

If you work with a cloned copy of your vault
([per-employee setup](/guides/per-employee-setup)), the vault is a plain
Obsidian vault — open it in Obsidian and its built-in graph view works with no
configuration, alongside backlinks, canvas, and every other Obsidian feature.

## The graph you see is the graph you're allowed to see

Both views draw from your compiled vault, and the
[compiler](/concepts/the-compiler) stubs out links that point at notes outside
your permissions — a note you can't read is never on your machine, so it can't
appear as a node or an edge. The graph isn't just a pretty view of your
knowledge; it's an exact picture of your slice of the company brain.
